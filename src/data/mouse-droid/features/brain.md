Everything you do gets quietly narrated into your Obsidian vault through the Mouse Droid
Memory service, so your day survives long after you've closed the laptop.

Mouse Droid works in **sessions**. When a stretch of focused work begins he opens a
session in your vault; as it goes he logs short narrated updates; and when you wind down
he writes a closing summary. Each of those entries carries **provenance** — it's stamped
as coming from Mouse Droid and tied to its session — so you can always trace a memory back
to the work that produced it.

Because it all lands in real Obsidian notes, the rest of the Memory Palace just works:
**recall** searches everything by meaning, project **hubs** gather a project's sessions
and notes in one place, and **links** keep it all connected. Turn on the optional extras
and he'll also keep each project's hub freshened with a rolling summary and log per-project
time as sessions close.
