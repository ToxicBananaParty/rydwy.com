Mouse Droid keeps a quiet eye on how long you've been heads-down and nudges you to step
away before you've been at it too long.

### Desk time and nudges

He tracks your **desk time** — continuous focus without a real break. A short idle gap or
locking your Mac counts as a break and resets the clock. Cross the break threshold
(**90 minutes** by default, adjustable in Settings) and he sends a gentle reminder to get
up. Stay glued for much longer and a firmer nudge follows. There's also an end-of-day
nudge once the evening rolls around and you're still working.

Nudges show up in the **Notifications** tab, and — in the installed app — as a macOS
notification. Opening the Notifications tab marks them as read.

With the Google Calendar connection and its own nudge switch on, he'll also
give you a quiet heads-up shortly before each meeting starts. The macOS toast
names the meeting title only — attendees and details never appear on the lock
screen; the full message waits in the **Notifications** tab.

### The wellness score

Alongside the nudges is a **0-10 wellness score**. It starts at 10 and eases down the
longer you go without a break (with a little extra ding for working into the evening),
shading from green to amber to red so you can read it at a glance. Take a break — or tap
**"I took a break"** on the Timeline — and it resets to a fresh 10.

_Nudges land in the **Notifications** tab; your score lives on the **Timeline**._
