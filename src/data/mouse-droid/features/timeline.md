The Timeline is where your day adds up. Mouse Droid takes the stream of window titles it
captures and turns it into something you can actually read.

**Day view** lays your activity out in time order, grouped into **sessions** — the
stretches of focused work between real breaks (a session ends when you lock the screen,
your Mac sleeps, or you step away long enough). Each session is a box headed with its time
span and duration.

Inside a session, runs of activity are labelled with the **project** you were working on —
inferred from your curated projects and tinted with a stable per-project color. When a
stretch spans several projects with no clear winner it shows as a **Mixed** band with a
chip per project; when nothing maps to a project it's quietly marked **No project**
(no guessing). Got one wrong? Use the small ✎ on a band to **reassign that span's
project** — pick another project, or clear it to **No project** — and the correction
sticks and survives re-inference. If project inference is off,
sessions still group your day; the bands simply fall back to a plain list.

Within each band, activity is bucketed into intervals. Switch the bucket size between
**1 hour**, **30 minutes**, **15 minutes**, or **none** (a flat, event-by-event list) —
sessions and bands wrap whichever grouping you pick. A bar across the top shows your
busiest apps for the whole day at a glance. Click any bucket to expand it — you'll get a
per-app breakdown with proportional bars and durations, and you can drill further into the
individual events that make it up.

**Meetings on the lane.** With the Google Calendar connection on, your day's
meetings appear right inside the Day view — indigo-tinted rows with a
**Meeting** chip, title, time range, and duration, placed chronologically
inside the session they overlap. A meeting with no captured activity around it
sits on its own between sessions — you were away. Session headers count their
meetings ("· 2 meetings"). Declined invitations and all-day events never show.
Off by default; nothing appears unless you connect a calendar in Settings →
Connections.

**Month view** zooms out to a calendar grid, with each day showing how your time split
across apps — handy for spotting patterns across a week or a sprint.

The day refreshes itself every few seconds, so it stays current while you work.

### The wellness panel

Pinned to the Timeline is a small wellness readout: your current **desk time** — how long
you've been heads-down without a real break — and an **"I took a break"** button. Tapping
it tells the droid you've stepped away: your desk-time counter resets to zero and your
wellness score springs back to a fresh 10. With Google Calendar connected, the panel also counts today's back-to-back
meetings.

_Find it in the **Timeline** tab._
